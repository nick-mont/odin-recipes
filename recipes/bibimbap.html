Bibimbap Recipe

Literally meaning 'mixed rice,' bibimbap is a popular meal in Korea where

you have white rice topped with vegetables, beef, a whole egg, and gochujang (red chili pepper paste).

If you don't want it to be spicy, you can change the gochujang for soy sauce or Sriracha.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup gochujang (optional)
  • 1 bunch of spinach, cut into thin strips
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 pinch of red pepper flakes
  • 1 pound of thinly-sliced beef
  • 4 large eggs
  • 4 cups of cooked white rice
  • 4 teaspoons of toasted sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Bring about 2 cups of water to a boil in a large nonstick skillet and stir in spinach
  2. Cook until spinach is bright green and wilted, 2 to 3 minutes
  3. Drain spinach and squeeze out as much moisture as possible
  4. Set spinach aside in a bowl and stir soy sauce into spinach.
  5. Heat 1 teaspoon olive oil in large nonstick skillet and cook and stir carrots until softened, about 3 minutes
  6. Stir in garlic and cook just until fragrant, about 1 more minute
  7. Sprinkle with red pepper flakes, and set the mixture aside in a bowl
  8. Brown beef in a clean nonstick skillet over medium heat, about 5 minutes per side, and set aside
  9. In another skillet, heat 1 more teaspoon olive oil over medium-low heat and fry the eggs to your liking
  10. To assemble the dish, divide cooked rice into 4 large serving bowls
  11. Top with spinach mixture, a few pieces of beef,and finish with the egg
  12. Drizzle each bowl with 1 teaspoon of sesame oil, a sprinkle of sesame seeds, and a small amount of gochujang paste if desired
